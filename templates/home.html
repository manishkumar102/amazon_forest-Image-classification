The ongoing fires burning in the Brazilian Amazon are assisting in confirming the findings of a new study published in July 2019 which shows a major interrelation between land usage and fire incidence â€” with deforestation and development adding more to fire occurrence than climate change . New research shows that unrestricted deforestation, along with the construction of new highways, could expand wildfire risk in the Amazon basin by more than 70 % by 2100, even inside fortified areas and aboriginal reserves that have relatively intact forests. Scientists suggest that attempts to improve sustainable land management and curtail future deforestation and development could offer the best defenses against the surging threat wildfires pose due to the increment in heat and drought brought by the increasing climate change.
